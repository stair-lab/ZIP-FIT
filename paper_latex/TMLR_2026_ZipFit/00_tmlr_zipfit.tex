\subsubsection*{Broader Impact Statement}
ZIP-FIT is a data-selection method: it uses gzip compression to choose training data aligned with a target task.
Cheaper, task-aware selection can lower the compute and energy cost of adapting language models and make high-quality data curation accessible without large embedding models.
The same targeting, however, inherits whatever biases or gaps are present in the target set used to drive selection, and could in principle be used to concentrate narrow or undesirable data; practitioners should audit both the target specification and the selected corpus, and respect the licensing and provenance of source data.
All datasets used in this work are publicly available.
